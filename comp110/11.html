This week introduces further concepts of vector maths. Vectors are used to represent positions and distances in 2D and 3D space, vital for almost all games.

This week we also look at how non-integer numbers are represented in computers. There are many subtleties to this, and understanding the quirks and limitations of floating-point representation is important to understanding any program that deals with numbers.

Learning Objectives:
  • Recall key concepts of vector mathematics
  • Calculate with vectors and apply calculations to real problems
  • Explain the IEEE floating-point number representation standard, and the implications of its use
